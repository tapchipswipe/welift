# Service Agreement — Liability & Risk Allocation Clauses (Draft)

**Use:** Starter language for a Virtual Gate Attendant Services Agreement with Florida HOAs / CDDs / management companies.  
**Status:** Draft for attorney review — **have Florida counsel licensed in community-association / security contracts customize before use.**

---

## Positioning statement (include in recitals)

Provider supplies **unarmed remote visitor verification and access-control support** according to Client’s written Post Orders. Provider does **not** provide police services, armed response, premises security guarantees, or a warranty that unauthorized entry will never occur (including tailgating).

---

## 1. Scope of services

1.1 Provider shall monitor designated entry points via video/audio during Scheduled Hours, verify visitors against Client-provided credentials/guest lists/resident authorizations, and remotely actuate Client’s gate controls when verification succeeds under Post Orders.

1.2 Provider shall log access events and make monthly summary reports available to Client.

1.3 Services **exclude**, unless added by written amendment: on-site guards, mobile patrol, alarm response, law-enforcement functions, maintenance of Client hardware, and force entry / physical detention.

---

## 2. Client responsibilities (critical for liability)

2.1 Client shall provide and maintain, at Client’s cost: functioning gates, locks, loops, cameras, intercoms, network connectivity, power, and backup power reasonably necessary for remote operation.

2.2 Client shall supply accurate Post Orders, guest lists, vendor schedules, trespass rules, and emergency contacts, and update them promptly.

2.3 Client acknowledges **tailgating, piggybacking, forced entry, and resident-propped gates** may defeat access controls; Client remains responsible for physical perimeter design and resident rules enforcement.

2.4 Client shall maintain its own property, directors & officers, and general liability insurance customary for Florida community associations.

---

## 3. Service levels (example — tune for overnight product)

3.1 Answer-time objective: Provider will use commercially reasonable efforts to answer interactive visitor sessions within **sixty (60) seconds** during Scheduled Hours, measured monthly.

3.2 Credits (optional): If monthly average answer time exceeds SLA, Client may receive a service credit of ___% of that month’s fee (sole remedy for SLA miss).

3.3 Planned maintenance / force majeure: network outages, Carrier ISP failures, hurricanes, and Client equipment failure suspend SLA.

---

## 4. Limitation of liability

4.1 **No guarantee of security.** Provider does not warrant that Services will prevent all unauthorized access, crime, injury, or property damage.

4.2 **Cap.** Provider’s aggregate liability arising out of this Agreement shall not exceed the fees paid by Client to Provider during the **twelve (12) months** preceding the claim (or $____, whichever is greater).

4.3 **Consequential damages waiver.** Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, exemplary, or punitive damages, including lost property value, assessment increases, or reputational harm, even if advised of the possibility.

4.4 **Exclusions from cap (negotiate carefully):** claims arising from Provider’s gross negligence or willful misconduct; uncapped IP indemnity if any — counsel to advise.

---

## 5. Indemnification

5.1 **Client indemnity.** Client shall indemnify and hold harmless Provider from claims arising out of: (a) Client’s defective or offline equipment; (b) inaccurate Post Orders or guest lists; (c) resident or guest conduct; (d) decisions Client mandates that conflict with law; (e) injuries occurring on Client property except to the extent caused by Provider’s gross negligence.

5.2 **Provider indemnity.** Provider shall indemnify Client from third-party claims for bodily injury or property damage to the extent caused by Provider’s negligent performance of the Services, subject to Section 4.

---

## 6. Insurance

6.1 Provider shall maintain during the Term:

- Commercial General Liability ≥ $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate  
- Professional Liability / E&O ≥ $1,000,000  
- Workers’ Compensation as required by Florida law  
- Cyber liability ≥ $1,000,000 (if storing video/PII)

6.2 Upon request, Provider shall furnish certificates naming **Association** and **Management Company** as additional insureds on GL (ongoing operations), with primary/noncontributory and waiver of subrogation where available.

6.3 Client shall maintain GL customary for associations and name Provider as additional insured if Client’s carrier allows (optional reciprocal).

---

## 7. Access decisions & escalation

7.1 Agents shall follow Post Orders. If Post Orders are silent or conflict, agents may deny entry and escalate to Client’s on-call contact.

7.2 Emergencies (fire, medical, active threat): agents shall contact **911** and Client’s emergency contact; Provider is not a first responder.

7.3 Suspected crimes in progress: observe/document, contact 911 as appropriate; do not instruct confrontation.

---

## 8. Data, video, and privacy

8.1 Video/audio may be recorded and retained for ___ days. Client is responsible for resident notices required under Florida law / governing documents.

8.2 Provider shall not sell personal data. Subprocessors (hosting, telephony) under written agreements.

8.3 On termination, Provider shall make available export of logs for ___ days, then delete per policy, unless legal hold.

---

## 9. Hardware & outages

9.1 If Client’s connectivity or gate hardware fails, Provider’s obligation is limited to notifying Client’s contacts and documenting the outage.

9.2 Fail-secure vs fail-safe gate behavior is Client’s engineering choice; Provider is not liable for municipal or life-safety configuration decisions.

---

## 10. Term, termination, transition

10.1 Month-to-month or ___-month initial term.  
10.2 Either party may terminate for material breach uncured after 30 days’ notice.  
10.3 Client may terminate for convenience with 30 days’ notice after month ___.  
10.4 Provider shall reasonably cooperate in transition to Client or successor vendor.

---

## 11. Regulatory

11.1 Provider represents it will maintain all licenses required under Florida law (including Chapter 493 Class B agency licensing if applicable) to perform the Services.

11.2 Individuals performing regulated security functions will hold required individual licenses.
